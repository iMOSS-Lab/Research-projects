# iMOSS Research Projects

Research projects developed within the **MOSS Laboratory**.

This repository serves as the **central catalogue of research initiatives** developed by the laboratory and its collaborators. Each project typically includes open-source computational tools, datasets, and documentation to support scientific studies in structural monitoring, smart materials, and data-driven engineering systems.

The iMOSS Lab develops methodologies combining:
- Structural Health Monitoring (SHM)  
- Structural dynamics and vibration analysis  
- Machine learning and data-driven system identification  
- Smart materials and metamaterials  
- Renewable energy structural systems  

Whenever possible, projects aim to support **open and reproducible research practices**.

---

# Research Areas
The laboratory currently develops projects in the following areas.

### Structural Health Monitoring
Development of vibration-based monitoring techniques for mechanical and civil engineering systems.

### Energy Systems Monitoring and Control
Analysis of operational and structural data from wind turbines to support fault detection and performance monitoring. Analysis and designing controls for overhead transmission lines.

### Smart Materials and Metamaterials
Design and analysis of engineered materials with tailored dynamic and wave propagation properties.

### Data-Driven Structural Dynamics
Integration of machine learning techniques with classical structural dynamics models.

# Active Research Projects

## Wind Energy Systems
- **[wedowind-challenge-ASCE-EMI](https://github.com/iMOSS-Lab/wedowind-challenge-ASCE-EMI)** : Open computational framework developed for the **WeDoWind Challenge** organised by the American Society of Civil Engineers (ASCE) through the Engineering Mechanics Institute (EMI). Awared solution.
The project focuses on the analysis of **wind turbine operational and structural monitoring data**, including: SCADA operational measurements, structural response signals, vibration-based monitoring data, and data-driven diagnostic techniques. The repository provides tools for **wind turbine monitoring, data processing, and structural dynamics analysis**.
